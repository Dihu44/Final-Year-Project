Botswana entry visa

Eligibility for a Botswana Visa

All Citizens of Commonwealth countries do not require visa except those from Bangladesh, Cameroon, Ghana, India, Nigeria, Pakistan and Sri Lanka. These include those who have permits, but decide to stay outside the country for more than six months are required to apply for a visa when they come back to Botswana. Foreign nationals whose countries have signed a Visa Abolition Agreement with Botswana are also not required to apply for a visa.

Requirements for applying for a Botswana Visa

  1. Fully completed study visa application document
  2. Certified/notarised Passport copy - must contain passport numbers, date of birth, expiry date of the passport etc.
  3. Two identical coloured and clear passport size photos (with a white background)
  4. Invitation letter from the host institution (invitation letter from the Office of International Education & Partnerships)
  5. Copy of airline travel itinerary
  6. Admission letter
  7. Sponsorship letter and bank statements of the sponsor (at least 1 month bank statement for self-sponsored students)

Where to apply for a visa

Botswana has 20 diplomatic missions in 17 countries around the world where visa applications can be made: Australia, Belgium, Brazil, China, Ethiopia, India, Japan, Kenya, Namibia, Nigeria, South Africa (Pretoria), Sweden, Switzerland, United Kingdom, United States (New York), Zambia and Zimbabwe.

How long does it take to get a visa?

A Botswana visa takes 7 - 14 working days to process.

Study permit and waiver

Application for a study permit or waiver will be processed only after admission and proof of academic registration at the University of Botswana.

Exchange students staying only one semester or up to 6 months need to apply for a residence waiver.

Requirements for a residence waiver include:

  1. Copy of UB admission letter
  2. Copy of sponsorship letter and a recent bank statement of the sponsor if, self-sponsored (free movers)
  3. Pages of passport containing passport numbers, date of birth, expiry date etc. (certified by the embassy or commissioning offices)
  4. Sponsors letter of support
  5. Students request letter for a waiver
  6. Cover letter in support of the studentâ€™s application by the International Education & Partnerships Office
